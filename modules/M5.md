
MODULE M5 — HAZARD COMMUNICATION & SDS
5-Minute Lesson Script

INTRODUCTION (20–30 seconds)
“Welcome to Module M5: Hazard Communication.
This training ensures you understand the chemicals in your workplace and how to stay safe.”

SECTION 1 — OSHA HAZCOM STANDARD (45 seconds)
“The Hazard Communication Standard gives you the Right to Know and the Right to Understand chemical hazards.
Labels and SDS sheets exist to keep you safe.”

SECTION 2 — LABELS, SDS, AND PICTOGRAMS (60 seconds)
“Chemical labels must include hazard statements, signal words, pictograms, and instructions.
SDS sheets contain detailed information about PPE, storage, first aid, and spill cleanup.
If you don’t know how to use a chemical — don’t guess. Check the SDS or ask.”

SECTION 3 — SAFE CHEMICAL PRACTICES (45 seconds)
“Never mix chemicals unless the label specifically says to do so.
Mixing bleach with ammonia or acidic cleaners can release toxic gas.
Store chemicals securely, use them in ventilated areas, and wear the correct PPE.”

SECTION 4 — SCENARIO CHECKPOINT (40 seconds)
“Scenario:
You see another employee mixing two cleaning chemicals to ‘make them stronger.’
Pause…
What is the correct response?
Stop the process immediately and notify a supervisor.
Mixing chemicals can produce dangerous gasses — sometimes deadly.”

CLOSING SUMMARY (30 seconds)
“To summarize:
Know the hazards.
Read the labels.
Use PPE.
Never mix chemicals.
Report missing labels or SDS sheets immediately.
When you’re ready, proceed to the Module M5 quiz.”

END OF MODULE M5
